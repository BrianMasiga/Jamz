HERE'S WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT RHUMBA

The "rumba influence" came in the 16th century with the black slaves imported from Africa.

  • The Rumba originated among the African slaves in Cuba in the sixteenth century.
  • Best known for the dancers' subtle side to side hip movements with the torso erect, the rumba is danced with a basic pattern of two quick side steps and a slow forward step.
  • Rumba, also spelled rhumba, ballroom dance of Afro-Cuban folk-dance origin that became internationally popular in the early 20th century.

The native Rumba folk dance is essentially a sex pantomime danced extremely fast with exaggerated hip movements and with a sensually aggressive attitude on the part of the man and a defensive attitude on the part of the woman.
